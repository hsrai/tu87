I feel greatly honoured at this yet another opportunity provided by the Alumni
Association through this photo galaxy 'Souvenir 1987' to salute the twenty-eighth
batch of our graduating class and to extend my heartiest felicitations to them.
It is a matter of immense satisfaction to see you arrive at the end of a grue-
lling struggle spread over four handsome years of your youthful era, bearing tre-
mendous strains of an academic programme that prepared you for a prestigious
career. A cherished dream has come true as you leave the portals of this premier
Institute, now hallowed by a new status of a Deemed University.
We are sure you must have taken full advantage of the multifarious educa-
, tional facilities which provide an environment for growing and maturing up fast,
for acquiring distinctive credentials and a profile that will help you and sustain
you in your future growth and recognition.

continued education, show up as a product of a culture and be a vessel of good
manners-the stuff that makes life worth living, cost so little but are precious
treasures. They are decided assets of any forceful magnetic personality, win
confidence and generate leadership every where.
This year's parting advise has been formulated as ten commandments for
civil behaviour: Be good natured, kind and courteous; exhibit patience good bree-
ding and a generous heart, practise respectfulness, cheerfulness and saintliness;
bear no malice, hatred or insult.
